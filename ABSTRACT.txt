Pre-registered Falsification of Timing-Based Divergence Decomposition in KV-Cache Compression

We report a pre-registered falsification of the "Two Regimes" hypothesis,
which proposed that compression-induced output divergence in LLM inference
decomposes into two (allegedly independent) axes: density (flip rate Phi)
and timing (Position of Double-Flip PDP_2). The hypothesis was formalized ex ante in
contract_validation.md v0.4 and frozen in git before any Phase 2 data
collection. Data: 3 models (mistral-7B-Instruct, qwen-7B-Instruct,
qwen-14B-Instruct), 2 datasets (synthetic, ShareGPT), 3 compression methods
(uniform, recent, h2o_approx), 4 retentions (0.2, 0.5, 0.8, 0.9), N=50
conversations per condition, T=128 generated tokens, RTX 3090 single GPU.
Analysis strictly follows the pre-registered decision rules: Bonferroni-
corrected Spearman for H1, KS-test per retention with strict all-pass rule
for H2, and grouped 5-fold CV (group=conversation_id, 30 repeats) for H3
with macro-F1 gain thresholds. Results: H1 KILL (rho < 0.3 on 4/9 cells,
driven by `recent` on all 3 models), H2 WEAK (only mistral passes strict
rule), H3 KILL (Delta macro-F1 < 0.05 on all 3 models; **strictly negative
on qwen-7B, CI95 = [-0.028, -0.011]**, indicating PDP is unstable and
occasionally harmful, not merely redundant). Outcome gate: PROJECT KILLED.
The bi-axial decomposition is therefore falsified; what survives is a
predominantly mono-dimensional density signal (Phi under `uniform`). One unexpected,
not-pre-registered observation is logged: `recent` exhibits a non-monotonic
"middle-gap" pattern on ShareGPT contexts across all 3 models, with Phi
peaking around r=0.5. Combined with the loss of KL_mean as a continuous
signal on qwen-7B-sharegpt (numerical underflow under 4-bit + eager + long
context) and asymmetric ShareGPT context truncation across models (1024 vs
512 tokens, imposed by 24 GB VRAM), this points away from "timing" and
toward "context structure" (position + role + local density) as the
plausible governing axis. A sketch of Hypothesis V2 along these lines is
included as future work, not as a claim. The archive includes the frozen
contract, code, raw results, environment dumps, and analysis logs
sufficient to reproduce all results.

Keywords: KV-cache compression, LLM inference, pre-registration,
falsification, flip rate, StreamingLLM, H2O, open science, reproducibility.
